The Brandeburg Gate

Completed in 1791, the Brandenburg gate is one of the most famous monuments in Germany. It was built on the orders of King Frederick William II to be the conclusion to the boulevard Unter den Linden (“Under the linden trees”).

This sandstone Gate stands at an impressive 26 meters tall and 65 meters wide, the horse drawn chariot named the “Quadriga” was placed on-top the gate in 1793, the statue was created by the artist Johann Gottfried Schadow. The statue depicts the goddess Victoria riding the chariot which is being drawn by 4 horses, the statue symbolizes peace for those entering the city.
