The German Oktoberfest

Oktoberfest is the most commonly known German tradition outside of Germany, largely because of their beer consumption during the festival. In 2013 the festival had an attendance of 6.4 million people, and the amount of beer consumed during said festival was 6.7 million liters of beer (wikipedia.com) which makes it about 1 liter drunk per person.

Although Oktoberfest is a German festival celebrated in the city of Munich which is about 600km south of Berlin, it is celebrated in different parts around the world which also holds the festival modeled after the traditional one held in Munich.
